History of Bareilly

Bareilly, founded in 1537 by Jagat Singh Katehriya, has a rich historical significance. It served as the capital of Rohilkhand region before becoming an important center of British administration.

The city played a crucial role during the Revolt of 1857 and was an important military station during British colonial rule. The historic landmarks still showcase the city's glorious past.

Today, Bareilly stands as a testament to the harmonious blend of its historical heritage and modern development as a smart city.

Introduction to Bareilly

Bareilly is a bustling metropolitan city in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. Located in the northern part of India, it serves as the administrative headquarters of Bareilly District and Bareilly Division.

With a population of over 1 million, Bareilly is one of the largest cities in Uttar Pradesh. The city has a diversified economic base including manufacturing, retail, and educational institutions.

Known for its unique mix of traditional and modern lifestyle, Bareilly offers a vibrant cultural scene along with all the amenities of a developed urban center.

What Bareilly is Famous For

Wooden Furniture & Handicrafts

Bareilly is renowned for its beautiful wooden furniture and handicraft items made from sheesham wood, exported worldwide.

Printing Industry

The city is a major hub for printing and publishing with numerous presses producing textbooks and other printed materials.

Bareilly Ka Peda

The city's famous peda sweet, with its unique flavor and texture, is a must-try for all visitors to Bareilly.

Transportation in Bareilly

Bareilly City

The city has a well-connected road network with buses, auto-rickshaws, and cycle rickshaws as primary modes of local transport.

Major routes connect all parts of the city, making commuting convenient for residents and visitors alike.

Bareilly Junction

Bareilly Junction is a major railway station in North India, connecting the city to Delhi, Lucknow, Mumbai, and other important destinations.

It serves as an important railhead for both passenger and freight services in the region.

Bareilly Bus Station

The main bus station provides road connectivity to nearby cities and towns through UPSRTC and private bus services.

Both ordinary and luxury buses operate from here, making it a major transport hub.
